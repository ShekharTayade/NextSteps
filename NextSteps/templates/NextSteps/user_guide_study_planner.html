Study Planner

Plan, Track and Manage Study Hours
You plan your study through the month(s), year(s). This is a tool that will keep you company through your study journey. Using the Study Planner you can plan your study hours well, track the progress and and graphically see where you stand.
Study Planner has an excellent graphical "Dashboard". It shows your progress with respect to the plan and gives insights that can be useful.

How to use Study Planner

Study Planner has a series of tabs. The first one is a "Dashboard" that graphically shows your progress. It doesn't need any input from you.
Start with "Overall Study Schedule" tab and enter the required details, then move on to "Subject Schedule" to break it down by subjects, then move on to "Day Schedule" tab to plan your typical day. When you have entered all the required details in these three tabs, you can generate your schedule calendar. The generated schedule calendar is available in "Schdule Calendar" tab where your further update/refine it as you like.
The last tab is "Log Study Hours". Use this tab to enter your actual study hours against your plan. When all the above details are entered the "Dashboard" will show your progress and show some tips for you.
  The typical sequence of your updates will be "Study Schedule" to enter overall study sch  "Suject Sch" to break it down by subjects   "Day Sch" to plan your typical study day.
When you make changes to any of "Study Schedule", "Subject Sch" or "Day Sch" tabs, please follow the sequence above and use the "Generate Schedule Calendar" button in "Day Sch" tab to generate the updated study calendar.
  • On the home page, click on “Study Planner” under NextSteps Features section.

Dashboard

  • The Dashboard is your snapshot of where you stand with respect to your plan.
  • This is a "display only" tab and doesn't need any input from you. When there is no plan or no actual data entered by you, it will not show the graphs and just
    • Overall Planned Vs Actual Hours shows, as of the date, the toal hours planned by you (since the beginning of your plan) and how many actual study hours logged by you
    Subject-wise Actual Study hours shows, as of the date, subjectwise break-up of actual study hours logged by you
    Current Month Actual Study hours shows actual study hours logged by you during current month
    Current Month Subject-wise Actual Study hours shows subject-wise actual study hours logged by you during current month

Study Schedule

  • This is where you put in your overall study plan. It will be the basis as you plan subject-wise and then plan your typical study day.
  • The top part shows your current study schedule. You can view your ealrier versions of the study schedule by clicking "View Your All Schedules Here" link. These can't be used though.
  • If you want to create a new schedule, then use "Create New Study Schedule" section.
  • When you are done answering the three questions, click on "Save" button.
  • This now becomes your new study schedule.

Subject Sch

  • Having planned for your overall studies, break it down subject-wise here.
  • Select subjects you plan to study, assign a percentage of your study time you want to dedicate to each subject and then define the start and end dates for each subject.
  • You can choose from the existing subjects. Click on the subjects that you plan to study. Then click on button to move selected subject(s) to "Subject Schedule" section.
  • If the subject you plan study is not listed, then click on "Add New Subject" button, enter the subject name and click OK. It will be added to "Subject Schedule" section.
  • Now, for each of the subject in "Subject Schedule" section, enter the percentage of time your want to allocate by clicking in "% Alloc" column.
  • Next, enter the start and end dates for each subject (please note that these dates should be within overall study schedule from previsous tab.
  • The total percentage allocation should not exceed 100%. Click on "Calculate %" button to verify it.
  • Once you have finshed entering all the details, click on "Save" button to save your changes.
  • Move on to "Day Sch" to plan your typical day.

Day Sch

  • Let's plan your typical study day here. You would have already entered your overall study schedule and then broken it down by subjects in Subject Sch tab.
  • In this tab, the left hand side section shows the subjects you have selected, start and end time and the hours. The right hand side graphically shows how does your day look like.
  • For each subject, enter the start and end time of the day, the planned hours will get calculated.
  • NOTE: PLEASE ENTER TIME IN 24 HR FORMAT. For an example 2:30 pm is entered as 14:30.
  • In the "Study Schedule" tab you mentioned how many days of the week you plan to study. In this tab, mention which are those days of the week. Check the checkbox for the desired day.
  • NOTE: THE NUMBER OF DAYS YOU SELECT SHOULD MATCH WITH NUMBER OF DAYS IN "STUDY SCHEDULE" TAB.
  • After the start and end time, planned hours are entered, the desired days are selected and you are satisfied with your planning, click on "Save" button to save your changes.
  • If you have latest changes for which the schedule calendar is not generated, then "Generate Sch Calendar" button will get enabled.
  • Click on "Generate Sch Calendar" button to generate the calendar. This will update calendar in "Sch Calendar" tab.
  • NOTE: FOR THE DAYS WHERE YOU HAVE ALREADY LOGGED ACTUAL HOURS WILL NOT BE AFFECTED AND SCHEDULE FOR THOSE DAYS WILL NOT BE CHANGED.

Log Study Hours

  • Use this tab to log your actual study hours against the planned hours.
  • Use this tab to log your actual study hours against the planned hours.
